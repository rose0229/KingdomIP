01 — The short version

What we found, in plain terms

You are a warm, deeply rooted church that the people who already attend genuinely love. The work isn't to become a different church online — it's to help a stranger find, trust, and take a first step toward the church members already know.

What's working

People already love you

A strong reputation (roughly 4.8 stars across 57 reviews), more than 80 years in Laveen, real sermons online, and a modern app for events and giving. The foundation is genuinely good.

What's holding you back

Strangers get a mixed signal

Your most useful "first visit" details are hard to find, your basic facts (like service times) disagree across the internet, and the loudest button you offer a newcomer is "Give."

The clearest opportunity

Build one clear front door

Give a curious newcomer one obvious, honest path: here's what Sunday is like, here's where your kids go, here's how to take a first step — the same story everywhere they look.

The heart of it

The opportunity isn't to do more ministry. It's to make the ministry you already have easier to discover, trust, and join.

02 — The first thing a newcomer sees

Your welcome speaks to people who already came

When someone curious lands on your "New Here?" page, it opens like a thank-you note written after a visit — "We are glad you joined us today" — and answers most questions by inviting them to call or stop by the office. For a first-timer deciding whether to come at all, that's a closed door, not an open one.

What's missing up front

The gap

The six questions a first-time guest actually has — what's it like, when do I arrive, where do I park, where do my kids go, how long is it, what do I wear — aren't answered together in one place.

The loudest button is "Give"

Needs attention

Across your site, the one prominent button is "Give Now." A newcomer who isn't ready to give yet has no equally clear "Plan your visit" step to take.

Everything routes to the office

Needs attention

Your kids and youth pages, when a parent has a question, point them to phone the office. Many first-time families won't make that call — they'll just move on.

04 — The clearest single problem

Your service times don't match across the internet

This is the most fixable, highest-stakes issue we found. A family deciding to visit this Sunday can land on four different versions of when you meet. Some say 9:00, some say 8:30; some show one service, some show two or three.

05 — Your sermons & worship online

The teaching is there. A newcomer just can't sample it.

Visitors increasingly "try" a church by watching before they attend. You're well-positioned for this — and a few small moves would turn a quiet archive into a welcome mat.

The strength

You already do the hard part

  • You livestream your Sunday service, in person and online.
  • You keep a library of past services on YouTube.
  • You even post adult Sunday School teaching — a rare, generous touch.
  • People mention strong preaching and a warm mix of modern and traditional music in their reviews.
The gap

A first-timer doesn't know where to start

  • There's no "watch this first" message, current series, or short welcome clip to sample.
  • Your YouTube page still shows old service times, so the storefront feels out of date.
  • The podcast trails off at 2024 with links "coming soon."
  • Nothing connects a sermon a stranger enjoys to a clear "now plan a visit" step.

09 — Three things worth fixing first

If you only do three things

Finding 01

Make one true story show up everywhere

What we saw
At least four different versions of your service times live online, plus an old site and directories listing former staff.
What it creates
A newcomer can't tell which is true — and may arrive at the wrong time, or quietly doubt the rest of what they read.
Why it matters
It silently undercuts the warm, trustworthy reputation you've already earned.
First move
Claim and correct your Google listing first, then your YouTube "About," then any directory you can find — all matching your website.
Finding 02

Turn "New Here?" into a real first-visit page

What we saw
The page welcomes people as if they've already come, and sends questions to the office phone.
What it creates
A curious family can't picture their Sunday — so the easiest choice is to not come.
Why it matters
This single page is where most first decisions are made.
First move
Add a short "Plan your visit" block answering six questions, and add a "Plan your visit" button next to "Give." (Copy you can paste is below.)
Finding 03

Help parents and seekers take the next step

What we saw
Kids and youth pages describe purpose well but skip arrival logistics; the youth events list is empty; sermons aren't tied to a next step.
What it creates
People who are interested still don't know what to do next without calling.
Why it matters
Families decide fast and protectively; missing logistics is a common reason a first visit never happens.
First move
Put check-in details on the kids page, the youth meeting time on the youth page, and a "plan a visit" link under your sermons.

Where Kingdom IP would start

A focused "Front Door" cleanup
